d:T167e,<p>This is the <strong>first devlog</strong> for the <strong>new project</strong> codenamed <strong>P2D</strong> I'm working on. It's still a prototype more than a game and I'm yet trying to find the correct mechanics for the gameplay experience I'm looking for.</p>
<h3 id="tldr"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#tldr"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>TL;DR</h3>
<p>I removed all my gamedev progress and started from zero.</p>
<p><strong>New mechanics!</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Movement</strong>: The player can move around with his legs.</li>
<li><strong>Jumping</strong>: The player jump around with his legs.</li>
<li><strong>Camera Follow</strong>: There will be some magic entity that will follow you very smoothly filming whatever you're doing.</li>
<li><strong>Health</strong>: The player have health, is a living thing</li>
<li><strong>Damage</strong>: The player can be damaged losing health</li>
<li><strong>Dead</strong>: The player can die respawning where he started the level</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>New gameplay experiences!</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Move and jump</strong> around the world with the smoothest <strong>camera follow</strong> ever.</li>
<li>Currently the only thing that could <strong>kill you</strong> is <strong>fall damage</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Scroll down to see some screenshots and GIFs!</p>
<h3 id="wiping-the-dust"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#wiping-the-dust"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>Wiping the Dust</h3>
<p>This project has some past. Here is some old stuff I did time ago before sticking to something simpler:</p>
<img src="/posts/assets/ghost-posts-images/2018/04/Concept.png" alt="Old Concept Art">
<p>I get to implement those tiles and the main character and some animations but the thing is that I last too much in finishing this assets and the feeling were of doing slow progress. So I decided to dump all the progress I did and start clean with a simpler idea and very basic mechanics.</p>
<h3 id="clean-start"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#clean-start"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>Clean Start</h3>
<p>I did a character concept with a small pixel ratio and a few animations, which let me spend less but enough time doing art:</p>
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/sDojV70.gif" alt="Player First Animations">
<p>And then I create a small level concept animated by hand:</p>
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/0U2gb8F.gif" alt="First Game Concept Animation">
<p>With this assets I could make a very very simple game, but was enough for me to keep with the project without losing enthusiasm. What I also did was spamming my friends and teammates with my progress in the game, this helped me a lot since any bit of attention were a pretty good point to continue with the project.</p>
<p>Also at the same time Unity released the <a href="https://unity3d.com/es/learn/tutorials/s/2d-game-kit" rel="nofollow">2D Game Kit</a> to help people without code knowledge start developing 2D games. Guess what? I have a small experience with Unity3D and C# programming but I took advantage of this and used some of their scripts to have some things working without losing too much time thinking, like the respawn logic or the scene controller.</p>
<h3 id="core-mechanics"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#core-mechanics"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>Core Mechanics</h3>
<p>I defined in a flowchart the <strong>core mechanics</strong> of the game (movement, jump, camera follow, health, damage and dead) with small descriptions of what I want this mechanics to be like. I also spend some time defining some game history like how the world is, what is going on or some superadvanced mechanics, just for fun and to see a bit of the big picture and not get bored with simpler stuff.</p>
<h3 id="results"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#results"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>Results</h3>
<p>With my core mechanics defined I started developing the game with some of the core mechanics. I've end up having a small prototype where you can move around with this character (name to be defined) jumping over a bunch of platforms made from the same tileset, testing how the game feels and gathering as much feedback as I can from the people that played it.</p>
<p>Once I had the health, damage and dead mechanics I implemented <strong>fall damage</strong>. If you fall from a great height you'll die:</p>
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/u6K4jyV.gif" alt="Implemented Fall Damage">
<p>Then I implemented level transitions to load new areas:</p>
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/cpdLGT6.gif" alt="Zone Swapping">
<p>And finally a small <strong>Start Menu</strong> to have something that feels more like a game:</p>
<img src="https://i.imgur.com/gXJXsZt.png" alt="Start Menu">
<h3 id="now-what"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#now-what"><span class="icon icon-link"></span></a>Now what?</h3>
<p>Now I iterate over and over and over until having a game. Next thing to make will probably be:</p>
<ul>
<li>Attack</li>
<li>Health Points</li>
<li>Destructibles</li>
<li>Collectables</li>
<li>A new tileset</li>
</ul>
